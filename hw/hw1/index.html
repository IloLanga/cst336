Definition

Malware is an abreviation from the word malicious software, it is any software specially design to cause damage to a computer, server, client or computer network, usually without the knowledge of the owner. Ransomware, computer worm and adware are three malware subsection.

History of Malware

1949 The theoretical preliminary work on computer viruses goes back as far as 1949. John von Neumann (1903-1957) developed the theory of self-reproducing automatons. However the details of the technical implementation were not conceivable at this time.
1981 Professor Leonard M. Adleman employs the term "computer virus" for the first time in conversation with Fred Cohen.
1983 In November Fred Cohen presents the concept of a virus for the first time in his seminar. For the implementation of a first functional virus running on UNIX, he needed only 8 hours. Within a few minutes he had full access rights to all computers.
